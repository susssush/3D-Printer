History Of 3D Printer

The concept of 3D printing began to take shape in the early 1980s. The first 3D printer Stereolithography (SLA) was invented by Charles Hull in the year 1983 and is considered one of the earliest 3D printing technologies. In 1988, Scott Crump invented Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) and The Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) and Electron Beam Melting (EBM) were developed in 1990. The RepRap project, initiated in the mid-2000 and the MakerBot Cupcake CNC, introduced in 2009, was one of the early consumer-grade 3D printers that contributed to the democratization of the technology.

Importance of 3D Printer


Medical Application - 3D printing is all set to revolutionize the medical and health field. Reduced Waste - Traditional subtractive manufacturing methods can produce significant waste. 3D printing is an additive process which minimizes material waste. It also helps to reduce the amount of distance that a product will travel. Rapid Prototyping- 3D printing allows for the quick and cost-effective creation of prototyping and iterations, which is invaluable for product development. Space Exploration- 3D printing is used to create tools and components in space, reducing the need to transport everything from Earth. Accessibility- Consumer-grade-3D printers have made the technology accessible to individuals, small businesses, and hobbyists. On -Demand Production- it enables just in time manufacturing , reducing the needs for large inventories and storage costs.
